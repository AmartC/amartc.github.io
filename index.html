ABOUT THE SHR COLLABORATIVE

OUR MISSION

Modernizing Healthcare

The SHR provides the foundation for the collection, communication, and aggregation of patient data, accelerating secondary uses in public health, disease surveillance, post-approval monitoring, and patient-centered outcomes research. By architecting a single set of standards for health-related data, the SHR can realize clinical interoperability and support a wide-range of clinicians, caregivers and healthcare providers.

Enabling transparency and precise communication across the healthcare system, the SHR will lead clinicians and the American public to realize major benefits through improved care coordination, reduction of medical errors, and decreased costs that accompany healthier lives.

THE SPEC BEHIND SHR

Prioritizing Primary and Emergency Care

The SHR specification will contain all information critical to patient identification, emergency and primary care. The collaborative is currently prototyping health record specifications that leverage existing medical record models and account for the data needs of patient identification and primary and emergency care providers. The SHR also actively addresses the dynamic needs of patients over the course of their life by including a specification for many areas related to social determinants of health

Future extensions will support emerging treatment paradigms such as genomics, microbiomics, and precision medicine. Patients own the information in their SHRs and control who can access them and contribute information.

THE SPECIFICATION

Patient-Clinician Needs The Foundation Of Health Data

Patient Identification

SHR will contain patient attributes like 'date of birth', 'last name', and 'place of birth' that make it easily searchable by an Electronic Master Patient Index (eMPI)

Emergency Care

The SHR will contain critical patient information, including advance directives, medications and allergies, that could impact emergency care.

Primary Care

Patient data is aggregated from sources like clinician visits, urgent care centers and patients themselves, providing a comprehensive patient view for clinicians and caretakers.

ABOUT THE SHR COLLABORATIVE

OUR MISSION

Modernizing Healthcare

The SHR provides the foundation for the collection, communication, and aggregation of patient data, accelerating secondary uses in public health, disease surveillance, post-approval monitoring, and patient-centered outcomes research. By architecting a single set of standards for health-related data, the SHR can realize clinical interoperability and support a wide-range of clinicians, caregivers and healthcare providers.

Enabling transparency and precise communication across the healthcare system, the SHR will lead clinicians and the American public to realize major benefits through improved care coordination, reduction of medical errors, and decreased costs that accompany healthier lives.

THE SPEC BEHIND SHR

Prioritizing High Quality Data

The SHR specification will contain all information critical to patient identification, emergency and primary care. The collaborative is currently prototyping health record specifications that leverage existing medical record models and account for the data needs of patient identification and primary and emergency care providers.

The SHR also actively addresses the dynamic needs of patients over the course of their life by capturing many areas related to social determinants of health. Future extensions will support emerging treatment paradigms such as genomics, microbiomics, and precision medicine. Patients own the information in their SHRs and control who can access them and contribute information.

HOW WE'RE DOING IT

Collaborating on a Global Scale

The development of the SHR and all resources are open source and have been since its inception. If you are interested in participating in the development of the SHR please email us here or enter an issue on the SHR GitHub page.

If you have questions for the collaborative about what makes the SHR unique, what separates the SHR from a personal health record and traditional EHR's, see our list of frequently asked questions or send us an email.

Can You Help Perfect The SHR?

Perspectives from all stakeholders in the healthcare community, from clinicians and policymakers to patients and informaticists, help ensure the SHR's accuracy. We want you to reach out, start a dialog, help us refine the SHR.

Domain Experts

Have you noticed clinical data that's missing from the current specification? Can you think of policies that could benefit from the SHR? Start a conversation with the collaborative and we'll help channel your expertise into the specification.

Standard Health Record Initiative

Our vision is to fundamentally shift how healthcare information is created. We are engineering this shift through defining the content of a Standard Health Record (SHR). A well-executed SHR will move the health system toward an integrated, personalized, prevention-focused, and modernized system.

The Standard Health Record Must:

Protect the security and privacy of patient health information.

Reduce waste, fraud, and abuse in healthcare delivery systems.

Provide secure, informed-consent access to patient health data across multiple platforms.

Allow modern healthcare systems and processes to improve the patient’s healthcare experience.

Empower the patient to add their own data, verify existing data, and share their data with trusted third parties.

Provide accessiblity by patients and authorized healthcare providers to the patient’s health-record world-wide.

The standard health record will:

  • Allow a single source of patient health information The SHR will provide a single source of information that contains the complete patient health data needed for patient identification, emergency care, and primary care.
  • Define core components The SHR will contain all the critical data needed to support patient identification, emergency care, and primary care for US civilian, DoD, and VA patients.
  • Improve information transfer The SHR will provide defined data elements, terminology mappings, and value sets. This will allow medical information to be stored, transferred, and merged using the SHR specification. It will leverage existing standards such as a constrained version of HL7 FHIR.
  • Follow informed practices The SHR will use internationally adopted best practices, taking cues from England’s Summary Care Record, Sweden’s National Patient Summary, and the ONC Common Clinical Data Set, among others.

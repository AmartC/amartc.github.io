The Standard Health Record Initiative

Our vision is to fundamentally shift how health information is created and shared within the US health system by defining a national Standard Health Record (SHR). The SHR provides a model for health data based on standard content, values, and measurement units. This standardization is foundational to creating a common operational picture of the patient that contains the right data.

Through the SHR, many of the challenges to successful health data exchange can be eliminated. With the right data at the right time, the SHR will support individual and population level care including improved care coordination, reduction of medical errors, and decreased costs.

The Standard Health Record is:

  • Patient-centric, logitudinal, and personalized
  • A simple, computable record that defines core human attributes for care
  • Understandable and verifiable by patients, clinicians, and algorithms
  • Open source software
  • Critical to solving healthcare semantic interoperability

The Standard Health Record is a MITRE Corporation initiative. MITRE is committed to adhering to its long-standing fiduciary role as a neutral and trusted advisor working in the public interest as a Federally Funded Research and Development Center (FFRDC) operator and partner supporting the CMS Alliance to Modernize Healthcare (CAMH).

Standard Health Record
Blue Ribbon Panel

The Standard Health Record (SHR) initiative will derive critical value from the guidance and support of a Blue Ribbon Panel. The Panel will serve for multiple years during the early stages of the initiative and contain experts representing clinical, patient advocacy, health information technology, health law and policy, and public health domains.

The Panel’s Charge

Panel members will provide guidance and support to the SHR development team in all aspects of carrying out the mission, vision, and goals of the SHR initiative. The Panel will be supported by, and have regular communication with, the SHR development team. The Panel may form workgroups from within and outside the Panel membership to carry out its charge.

About MITRE

MITRE is a not-for-profit organization that operates research and development centers sponsored by the federal government. MITRE operate FFRDCs, federally funded research and development centers, which are unique organizations that assist the United States government with Scientific research and analysis, Development and acquisition, and Systems engineering and integration. MITRE also has an independent research program that explores new and expanded uses of technologies to solve national problems in the public interest.

The CMS Alliance to Modernize Healthcare (CAMH) FFRDC, sponsored by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS), assists both the CMS and the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) in performing research and analysis on various topics that fall within the national health domain. As the CAMH FFRDC operator and partner, MITRE serves as an objective, independent advisor to CMS and HHS operating divisions. As the only broadly healthcare-focused FFRDC, CAMH supports many of the complex analysis and critical thinking requirements that encompass the business, policy, technology, and operational interests across CMS and HHS.
